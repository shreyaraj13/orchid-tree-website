Weekend Getaways Near Bangalore, the Slow Kind

13 June 2026  ·  6 min read  ·  By Orchid Tree

There are two kinds of weekend getaways near Bangalore. The kind where you drive three hours, tick off five viewpoints, and come back needing a holiday from your holiday. And the slow kind, where you go forty-five minutes up the road, walk into a forest, and do almost nothing at all. This is a note on the second kind, and why it might be the one you actually need.

Bangalore is a wonderful city to leave for a couple of days. The traffic, the calendars, the group chats that never stop. What most of us are looking for on a weekend is not another itinerary. It is quiet. And quiet, it turns out, is closer than you think.

Why slow beats packed

A packed getaway gives you photographs. A slow one gives you your attention back. When there is nothing to rush to, the morning stretches, a meal becomes the event, and you notice things, birdsong, the way the light moves through leaves, the fact that you have stopped reaching for your phone. The science is on the side of slowness too, time in nature lowers the stress hormones the city quietly winds up. You do not need a packed schedule for that. You need the opposite.

The best thing to do on a slow weekend is beautifully little.

Where it is, and how to get there

Orchid Tree sits in Yelachamanahalli Village, near Hoskote, in Bangalore Rural. From Whitefield it is about forty-five minutes by road, which is the sweet spot for a getaway near Bangalore, far enough that phones go quiet, close enough that you are not spending the weekend in the car. You can leave after a slow breakfast on Saturday and be back for Sunday dinner at home, rested rather than wrecked.

What a slow weekend actually looks like

Here is the whole agenda, and the point is that there is not one. Wake without an alarm. Eat a breakfast you did not have to plan. Float in the ozone pool until your fingers wrinkle. Take the buggy down to the farm and pull a tomato off the vine. Lose an afternoon to a paperback and a steam room. As the light drops, there is a bonfire, a sky thick with stars, and your own bottle poured slowly. You can read more about the things to do across the estate, but the honest answer is, do as much or as little as you like. Both are correct.

Where you stay

There are eleven rooms, and no two are the same. Some sit by the pool, bright and easy, made for sleeping in. Others are cottages built around living trees, with baths open to the sky and a private steam room. Couples lean to the romantic cottages, families and groups to the larger rooms. Whichever you choose, breakfast and the run of the estate come with it. Have a look at the stays and the rooms to find the one that fits the weekend you are after.

What you eat

Food here is part of the rest, not a sideshow. Breakfast is included and cooked fresh. Lunch and dinner are plated and pre-ordered, North Indian and Nepali home cooking, much of it grown on the estate farm a kilometre away. No buffet trays, no warming lamps. The estate is proudly BYOB, so you bring your own bottle and pour it under the stars. There is more on the restaurant and how the kitchen works if you like to plan your meals before you arrive.

Good for couples, families and friends

Pet-friendly cottages and family rooms, quiet couple rooms, and the whole estate available as a private buyout for a group. Calm is the constant, whoever you bring.

The best time to go

Honestly, any weekend. Winter brings cool, clear nights for the bonfire. The monsoon turns the forest green and the air soft, with hot pakodas and ginger chai for grey skies. Summer means long, lazy hours in the pool and mango finding its way into everything. A slow getaway does not really have a wrong season, only different moods of the same quiet.
