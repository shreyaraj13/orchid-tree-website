Section 01

Voice & Tone

Write like a calm, confident host, not a marketer. Every line must pass the only-Orchid-Tree test: could it appear on any other resort's site? If yes, rewrite it.

01
The Hard Rules
×

No exclamation marks. Anywhere.

Not in headlines, not in emails, not in captions. Calm confidence does not shout. If a line needs emphasis, choose stronger words.

×

No em dashes or en dashes.

Neither the em dash (—) nor the en dash (–) appears anywhere in Orchid Tree copy. Use a comma or a full stop instead.

OT

Always "Orchid Tree", two words.

Never "Orchidtree", never "orchid tree", never shortened to anything. The two words travel together, always capitalised.

Five Principles

Feeling before facts.

Open every section with the experience, then introduce the detail. "Wake to the pool and a skylight full of morning" comes before "Pool view suite, 380 sq ft."

One sensory idea per section.

Pick one specific, physical detail and commit to it. Morning light. The smell of rain on the farm. A candle-lit table under the tamarind. Not all three.

Sparse. Trust the photos.

Short sentences. Cut adjectives that do not add information. If the image shows it, do not describe it. If you need more than two sentences, cut one.

The only-Orchid-Tree test.

Before publishing any line, ask: could this appear on any other resort's site? If the answer is yes, rewrite it until it names the forest, the eleven rooms, the open-to-sky baths, or Mary.

Calm, and a little dry.

Confident without boasting. Warm without gushing. A note of quiet wit is welcome. Earnest sincerity is fine. Marketese is not.

Applied

These five principles apply equally to headlines, body copy, captions, WhatsApp messages, and the text inside buttons. The brand voice does not vary by channel.

Good Copy versus Copy to Avoid
✓
"Wake to the pool and a skylight full of morning."

Opens with a sensory moment. Specific to Orchid Tree. No adjectives doing double duty.

×
"Experience the ultimate luxury resort getaway with world-class amenities."

Passes on every other resort in India. Contains three hollow nouns in one sentence.

✓
"Eleven rooms. A forest. Forty-five minutes from Whitefield."

Facts as poetry. The short sentence rhythm signals calm confidence. Numbers in sans or spelled.

×
"We offer 11 rooms in a pristine natural setting perfect for your next getaway."

Passive construction, filler adjectives, and "your next getaway" belongs nowhere near Orchid Tree.

✓
"Dinner is plated, farm-to-table, and pre-ordered. There is no buffet here."

Honest and specific. The last sentence signals curation without arrogance.

×
"Enjoy an all-inclusive dining experience with all meals included in your package."

Both "all-inclusive" and "all meals included" are banned claims. And "enjoy" is a filler verb.

✓
"Add a massage whenever you like. The therapist comes to the room."

Factual, warm, specific. Correctly frames massage as an optional add-on.

×
"Includes a nightly massage and unlimited spa treatments for all guests."

"Nightly massage" is an explicit banned claim. "Unlimited" is never accurate here.

The Food Model

Breakfast Included with all overnight stays.
Lunch and Dinner Plated, farm-to-table, pre-ordered. Never buffet.
Drinks Proudly BYOB. No in-house bar.
Massage Available to add, whenever you like. Not included.

These phrases must never appear in any Orchid Tree copy, across the website, WhatsApp, email, or social channels.

Section 02

Photography & Imagery

Photography carries the brand. It must feel real, warm, and unstaged. Warm tones. Soft scrims. Real people and real emotion over staged venue shots.

02
Brand Photography Treatment

Each tile above is a clearly-commented one-line-swap slot. Replace src with the correct Orchid Tree asset. Never pull from Pinterest.

Use

  • Orchid Tree's own licensed photos first, always.
  • Real people, real emotion: a couple laughing, a sunrise stretch, a candle-lit table.
  • Big, appetising food photos with minimal text. No PDF menus.
  • Warm tones. Soft dark scrims for text legibility.
  • Gentle hover zoom (scale 1.04). Faint film grain on cinematic pages.
  • Descriptive, specific alt text on every meaningful image.
  • Unsplash or Pexels only for licensed placeholders, clearly labelled.

Never use

  • Pinterest or any unlicensed source.
  • Staged venue stock with gold-foil balloon setups.
  • Beach-cliché frames or generic-stock "luxury" imagery.
  • PDF menus or data tables in place of food photography.
  • Cool-toned or clinical photo edits.
  • mix-blend-mode on full-screen overlays (repaints every frame).
  • Stacked backdrop-filter blur over moving parallax.

Placeholder convention

Every placeholder image must carry a one-line HTML comment immediately above the <img> tag in the format: <!-- Replace with: [description of the real Orchid Tree photo needed] -->. The search mood and intended subject belong in the comment.

Section 04

SEO & Technical Standards

Every public page must be complete and correct before it ships. The checklist below applies to all guest-facing HTML files.

04
Per-page Checklist

Unique title and meta description

Target the page's real search intent. No boilerplate.

Canonical URL and robots tag

Guest flows and dashboards use noindex. Public pages: index, follow.

Open Graph + Twitter card tags

Include og:image with a representative photo (1200 x 630px).

JSON-LD structured data

Resort/LodgingBusiness, Restaurant, Service, auto FAQPage from visible Q&As.

Semantic HTML

header, nav, main, section[aria-label], article, figure/figcaption. Correct heading order.

Descriptive alt text

Every meaningful image. Decorative images use alt="".

Image performance attributes

decoding="async" on all images. loading="lazy" below the fold. fetchpriority="high" on hero only.

Reduced motion

All animations respect prefers-reduced-motion: reduce.

JSON-LD Convention

The FAQ accordion auto-generates FAQPage structured data from visible Q&As. This means the markup and the structured data can never drift apart. The script reads all .faq-q and .faq-a elements at runtime and writes the JSON-LD block to the document head.

Section 05

Content & Data Architecture

The site is static HTML, but content is data-driven from shared modules. Edit a value once in the module and every surface that depends on it updates.

05
shared/catalog.js

Rooms Single Source

The single source of truth for all eleven rooms across four types: copy, facts, amenities, photo galleries, and pricing. Edit a room type here and every page that references it updates automatically.

Stays page Home cards Booking flow
shared/menu.js

Dining Single Source

Categories, dishes, today's specials, seasonal items, always-available offerings, signature beverages, celebration add-ons, and dietary preferences. Powers three distinct surfaces from one file.

Restaurant page Plan Your Dining Kitchen dashboard

Production Direction

These front-end modules should be backed by a real backend (such as Supabase) so that the team updates a value once and the website, the guest pre-order link, and the kitchen dashboard all sync automatically. Currently these are static JS files. Any backend migration must preserve the same module API so page templates require no changes.

Section 07

Do & Don't

Quick reference. When uncertain, consult the fuller sections above. These are the distilled rules in two columns.

07
Do
✓

Lead with a feeling, then one sensory detail.

✓

Use the design tokens and existing components. Match the live site.

✓

Let photos carry the page. Keep body text sparse.

✓

Spell big numbers or set them in the sans typeface.

✓

Give every placeholder image a one-line swap comment.

✓

Respect prefers-reduced-motion. Keep scroll smooth.

✓

Use only Unsplash or Pexels for licensed placeholder photography.

✓

Place WhatsApp CTA as a nav pill and as a floating FAB past the hero.

Don't
×

No exclamation marks. No em dashes. No en dashes. No "Orchidtree" as one word.

×

No buffet, all-inclusive, or nightly-massage claims.

×

No Pinterest or unlicensed image sources.

×

No PDF menus, no data tables, no long unbroken paragraphs.

×

No cool forest green (#15583a). No large multi-digit numbers in Cormorant Garamond.

×

No mix-blend-mode on full-screen overlays. No stacked backdrop-filter over parallax.

×

No loud, gold-foil, banquet-factory, or generic-stock visual energy.

×

No copy that could appear on any other resort's site. If it passes, rewrite it.

Section 08

Deployment

Hosted on Vercel. One script handles the full deploy. Adding a new page is a one-line change to the PAGES array.

08

Production URL

orchid-sigma.vercel.app

1
Run ./deploy.sh from the project root. The script syncs all listed pages and shared/*.js into the linked .deploy/ folder.
2
The script sets / to the latest home.html and ships to production via the Vercel CLI.
3
To add a new page: add the filename to the PAGES array in deploy.sh. Nothing else changes.
4
Guest flows, dashboards, and app pages must carry <meta name="robots" content="noindex"> before they ship.
