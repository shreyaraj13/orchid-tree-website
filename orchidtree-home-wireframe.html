This is not a resort

Eleven rooms. One living forest. No itinerary.

Orchid Tree was not built on a plot of land. It was built around one, a forest left standing, with rooms tucked between the trees instead of clearing them away. There are no buffet halls, no event crowds, no schedule pinned to your door. You arrive, you slow down, and the estate takes it from there. Forty-five minutes from Whitefield, and a world away from it.

Estate / pool image
A day here

Mornings you don't set an alarm for

The day starts with birdsong and a breakfast you didn't have to plan. After that, the hours are yours. Float in the ozone pool until your fingers wrinkle. Take the buggy down to the farm and pull a tomato off the vine. Lose an afternoon to carrom and a paperback. As the light drops, there's a bonfire, a sky thick with stars, and your own bottle poured slowly. Do all of it. Do none of it. Both are the right answer here.

The garden cottages

A room with the sky for a ceiling

Chandana, Bodhi Tree and Hattimara are built around living trees. You bathe under open sky, wake to the canopy, and step onto a patio that belongs only to you. These are not poolside rooms. They are their own world, and the guests who choose them rarely want to leave.

The architect of stillness

Designed by Pradeep Kuppa Swamy

The landscape architect behind Infosys Mysore, Biocon and Britannia, with work recognised by the World Architecture Award. Orchid Tree is his reflection on stillness.

The architecture bends around the living forest, never the other way. Solar powered, sustained by recycled water, built with a minimal concrete footprint. When you breathe here, you breathe easy.
