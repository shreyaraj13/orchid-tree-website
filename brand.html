The "only Orchid Tree" test

Before publishing any line, ask: could this sentence appear on any other resort's site? If yes, rewrite it until it could only be us. The forest. The eleven rooms. The open-to-sky baths. Mary the host. The farm. The bonfire. Specificity is credibility.

Section 02

Logo

The mark is the orchid-tree spiral, paired with the name. One version, two contexts: light surfaces and dark surfaces. The script wordmark is a secondary signature, not a replacement.

On light / full colour

On dark / inverted white

On dark or over photos

filter: brightness(0) invert(1) renders the mark white. Add a soft drop-shadow for legibility.

On light or solid nav

Full colour, no filter applied. The warm brown and cream tones carry naturally on light backgrounds.

Sizing

Nav hero: ~72px. Solidified nav: ~52px. Footer: ~64px, inverted white.

Script wordmark

Sacramento "Orchid Tree" is a secondary signature. Footers and brand moments. It complements the logo, it does not replace it.

Section 03

Colour

Warm and earthy. A cream field, a deep olive brand colour, and a gold accent, all anchored by a family of warm browns that do most of the quiet work. Gold and olive are the jewellery, cream is the air, brown is the earth. Use CSS variables always. Never hard-coded hex in new work.

The warm brown family, the anchor

Brown does most of the quiet work on the site, the headings, the dark bands, the Stays footer, and the translucent wash over every photo. Most surfaces that read as dark here are not black, they are one of these browns. Reach for brown, not grey or black, for any shade, scrim, or shadow.

Contrast

Both primary text pairings pass WCAG AA with room to spare.

White on olive The forest, the eleven rooms, the open-to-sky baths. 9.6 : 1 contrast ratio / WCAG AAA
Ink on cream The forest, the eleven rooms, the open-to-sky baths. 7.4 : 1 contrast ratio / WCAG AAA

Colour history

Forest green
#15583a
Too cool, too clinical
→
Olive
#3c4a16
Warm, high-contrast, correct

The brand colour started as a deep forest green, which read cool and clinical against the warm cream. Moving to olive added warmth and, importantly, contrast: white text on olive is approximately 9.6:1, well past WCAG AA. Do not return to the cool green.

Eyebrow label

This is how a section opens.

The gold eyebrow precedes nearly every section heading. It orients, it doesn't shout. Always uppercase, 11px, 4px letter-spacing, gold, weight 700, with a 34px hairline before it.

CSS

Sacramento / --script / Secondary signature only. Never for body copy.

Numerals rule

Cormorant's figures are thin verticals. Big numbers in Cormorant read as Roman numerals. Always spell or use the sans.

Numerals in context
Avoid 11

Big Cormorant "11" reads as "II" or just 2. The thin serifs collapse at display size.

Preferred 11

Same number in Mulish, weight 700. Instantly legible. Use for all stat bands and large counts.

Also works Eleven

Spell it in Cormorant italic. The word reads clearly and carries editorial warmth.

Section 05

Spacing, layout and shape

Generous negative space is part of the brand. When unsure, add air. Asymmetry over symmetry. Mobile-first, always.

Page padding

--pad: clamp(24px, 5vw, 80px)

The standard horizontal gutter. Applied to every section. Scales fluidly from mobile to wide screens.

Content max width

1320px (.wrap)

All text and content stays within this container, centred. Images can optionally bleed to the viewport.

Section vertical rhythm

96px desktop / 60px mobile

Editorial sections breathe more: clamp(80px,14vh,160px).

Touch targets

44px minimum

Every interactive element meets the 44px minimum touch target on mobile. No exceptions.

Shape / radius
border-radius: 12px Cards, media, panels
--radius
border-radius: 8px Buttons, chips, inputs
--radius-sm
border-radius: 4px Cinematic pages, tighter feel
border-radius: 0 Full-bleed image sections, bands
Layout principles

Breathing room

Generous padding is not wasted space. It signals premium. When unsure, add more air.

Asymmetry

Splits are intentionally uneven, typically 46 to 54 percent. Headlines left, paragraphs offset. Symmetry reads as template.

Mobile-first

Everything collapses to a single column at 820px. Design for the phone screen, then expand for desktop.

No mix-blend-mode

Avoid mix-blend-mode on full-screen overlays. It repaints the entire page every frame. Use near-opaque fills instead.
